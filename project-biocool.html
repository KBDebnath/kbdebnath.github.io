BioCOOL

Data-driven Bio-fabricated building skins for passive cooling.

As our planet warms, the demand for air conditioning is skyrocketing, leading to higher energy bills and increased carbon emissions. BioCOOL reimagines the "skin" of our buildings as a living, breathing shield that cools our indoor spaces naturally.

Nature-Grown

We use mycelium—the root structure of fungi—to turn agricultural waste into high-performance building components. These materials are carbon-negative, meaning they store more carbon than they emit during production.

Zero-Energy Cooling

Inspired by vernacular "jaali" screens, BioCOOL uses porous geometries that facilitate natural airflow and moisture-driven cooling, reducing the reliance on mechanical air conditioning.

Climate Resilience

Our research focuses on extreme heat resilience, ensuring that future homes remain comfortable even during prolonged heatwaves in warming climates like Australia.

Data-Driven Design

By monitoring real-world occupant behavior and using advanced simulations, we tailor the building skin to respond to specific environmental conditions.

The Impact

BioCOOL is more than just a material; it is a shift toward Bio-Building Physics. By integrating biology with architectural technology, we aim to lower the carbon footprint of the construction industry while providing equitable, low-cost cooling solutions for everyone.
